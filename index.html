About North Carolina

A state built for newcomers

North Carolina gives newcomers a rare mix: fast-growing cities, accessible suburbs, university towns, mountain communities, and coastal escapes. It is practical enough for work and family life, but varied enough to offer very different lifestyles across the same state.

Use this guide to compare regions, understand daily-life basics, and get oriented around schools, housing, healthcare, transport, jobs, documents, and community support.

RTP
Research Triangle Park anchors tech, biotech, pharma, and university talent.
CLT
Charlotte is a major banking, logistics, sports, and corporate hub.
OBX
The Outer Banks and coast add lifestyle, tourism, and seasonal opportunity.
AVL
Asheville and the mountains offer culture, outdoors, and smaller-city living.

First-week essentials

Get oriented before you arrive

The first weeks are easier when you know where to shop, how to handle basic paperwork, what local terms mean, and which services people use day to day.

Groceries and household basics

Food Lion, Harris Teeter, Publix, Aldi, Walmart, Target, Costco, Sam's Club, and local farmers markets cover most everyday needs.

Pharmacy and home improvement

CVS, Walgreens, and Rite Aid handle prescriptions and daily health items. Lowe's, Home Depot, and local hardware stores cover repairs and home setup.

Documents and accounts

Plan around driver's license steps, vehicle registration, Social Security, banking, insurance, utilities, phone service, and proof-of-address paperwork.

Local shorthand

OBX means the Outer Banks, the Triangle means Raleigh-Durham-Chapel Hill, the Triad means Greensboro-Winston-Salem-High Point, and Tar Heel is part of the local vocabulary.
